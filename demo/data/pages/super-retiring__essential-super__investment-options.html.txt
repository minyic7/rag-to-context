## Lifestage investment options
When you open an Essential Super account, your money is automatically invested in one of the Lifestage options, designed to adjust your investment mix as you age. This can also be referred to as a MySuper Lifecycle option. At a younger age, when you have more time until retirement, a greater portion of your super is invested in growth assets such as property and shares. These assets can be more volatile but offer strong potential for higher long-term returns. As you approach retirement, your super automatically shifts towards defensive assets like cash and fixed interest. This helps protect your super balance from market volatility. Explore options
When you open an Essential Super account, your money is automatically invested in one of the Lifestage options, designed to adjust your investment mix as you age. This can also be referred to as a MySuper Lifecycle option. At a younger age, when you have more time until retirement, a greater portion of your super is invested in growth assets such as property and shares.
These assets can be more volatile but offer strong potential for higher long-term returns. As you approach retirement, your super automatically shifts towards defensive assets like cash and fixed interest. This helps protect your super balance from market volatility.

## Case study: Manraj
Manraj was born in 1998 and wants a hands-off approach to his super, so he chooses to leave the investing mix to the experts at CFS. This means he is invested in the Lifestage 1995-99 option. Since Manraj is a long way from retirement, his money is in a higher risk investment strategy as he has time to ride the waves of the volatile market. As he approaches retirement, CFS will gradually update his investment strategy to a lower risk one to help protect his returns gained over the years. With the Lifestage option, Manraj won't have to do anything except watch his super balance grow in the CommBank app.

## FAQs

## Which is the best super investment option?
This greatly depends on your long-term goals, tolerance for risk and investment time horizon.
Global Shares: More suited for members with capital growth goals over the long-term. This option is typically impacted by increased market volatility in bad years.
Balanced: Provides a mix of growth and defensive investments, and may suit a broad base of members.
Lifestage: This is a default option which automatically adjusts risk as you age. More suited for those wanting a ‘hands-off’ approach to investing.
Cash: More suited for those wanting stable investment returns, and are avoidant of capital loss.
Comparing options and finding those which align with your personal circumstances is key.

## How do I choose a super investment option?
Choosing the right super investment option starts with understanding your goals, comfort with risk, and investment timeframe. Here are a few pointers to guide your decision:
Consider your time horizon. If retirement is decades away, you may be more comfortable with higher-risk options that aim for long-term growth.
Think about diversification. Some options spread investments across multiple asset classes to balance risk and return, whilst others are more focused (e.g. shares, property).
Consider what's important to you. For example, some options avoid investments in companies that have an adverse effect on the environment.
Taking the time to compare options and understand what each offers can help you make a confident and informed choice that supports your financial future.

## Should I change my super investment option?
Before making a move, it’s important to understand how investing in super works and to consider your own investment goals and risk appetite. Each option should have its own Investment Fact Sheet which will show:
How each option has performed over time
The types of assets it invests in (such as shares, property, or cash)
The level of risk involved
These insights can help you make a more informed and confident decision.
