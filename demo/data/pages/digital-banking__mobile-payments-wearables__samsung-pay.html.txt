## Flexible ways to pay
Choose between Mastercard/Visa and eftpos payment options on your debit card.
Pay using your debit account through the Mastercard/Visa network in Australia and overseas. Payments will show up as pending in your account until fully processed.
Select ’eftpos’ to pay using your debit account through the eftpos network. Get cash out when you tap with no pending transactions. Only available in Australia.

## How to set up Samsung Pay on your mobile
Samsung Pay comes pre-installed on most recent Samsung phones. If you don't have the app, you can download it from the Google Play store
Sign in to your Samsung account and choose your verification method
Use your phone's camera to capture your card information or manually add the details
For security purposes, choose between receiving an SMS verification code or logging into the CommBank app to verify your identity
You’re all set to start using Samsung Pay

## How to set up Samsung Pay on your Samsung wearable
From any screen, press and hold the Back key on the Gear S3 for 1-2 seconds
Rotate the bezel to the right to view basic instructions, then tap the arrow to start the set up process
Sign into your Samsung account (if you haven't already). Tap OK to continue, then tap START
To add a payment card, tap ADD CARD
Tap TURN ON to set up a secure screen lock (if you don't already have one set)
On your Gear S3, enter a four-digit PIN, then re-enter to confirm it

## Support
Which devices are eligible for Samsung Pay? Samsung Pay is compatible with all Galaxy S6, Galaxy S7, Galaxy S8 and Galaxy S9 models as well as Gear S2 and S3 smart watches. See the full list of devices . Which cards are eligible? You can add your CommBank Debit Mastercard, CommBank World Debit Mastercard™, personal and business credit cards, corporate credit and charge cards and Visa Business Debit card. If you are an additional cardholder, you may add your eligible Commonwealth Bank credit card to Samsung Pay provided it is Mastercard branded. Visa credit cards issued to additional cardholders may be eligible for Samsung Pay, however, a transaction limit of $100 will apply. What happens if my Samsung device is lost or stolen? If your device is lost or stolen, you can use Samsung’s Find My Mobile service to remotely lock or wipe your device. This will lock or wipe Samsung Pay as well as your registered cards 2 . Alternatively, contact us on 13 2221 and we will delete your cards in the Samsung Pay app. How do I add eftpos to Samsung Pay? If you've added your CommBank debit card to Samsung Pay and don’t have the eftpos payment option, remove and re-add it to start using the eftpos network. You can then get cash out with no pending transactions when you pay by choosing eftpos. If you're using Samsung Pay for the first time, or adding a new debit card, the eftpos network will automatically be added to your CommBank debit card. Can I get cash out using Samsung Pay? Yes, you can get cash out using Samsung Pay. Simply select the ‘eftpos’ payment option before using your CommBank debit card to make a payment with Samsung Pay. This will update your default payment option for next time you make a purchase. Read more FAQs

## How secure are Samsung Pay transactions?
When making payments using Samsung Pay, your CommBank card number is not stored on your device or shared with merchants. Merchants will only see your Virtual Account Number, which is an alternative to your card number. It’s a way of protecting your card details so they aren’t exposed to anyone other than you. If you suspect a fraudulent transaction has occurred on your account, please contact us on 13 2221 immediately. When it comes to payment security, CommBank is a leader in fraud protection. So if we identify any unusual activity, you’ll be alerted immediately.
