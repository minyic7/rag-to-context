## What are the different types of cover?
You can choose from Income Protection and Life Cover. You can also add Total and Permanent Disablement (TPD) and Crisis Recovery (Trauma) cover to your Life Cover policy. Each serve a different purpose:

## Income Protection
Provides a monthly payment of up to 70% of your income if you're unable to work due to sickness or injury. 1 Premiums may be tax-deductible.

## Life Cover
Provides a lump sum payment if you pass away or are diagnosed with a terminal illness.

## Total and Permanent Disablement (TPD) cover
Total and Permanent Disablement (TPD) cover
Optional add-on to your Life Cover policy
Provides a lump sum payment to support you if you become totally and permanently disabled and are unable to work. 5

## Crisis Recovery (Trauma) cover
Crisis Recovery (Trauma) cover
Optional add-on to your Life Cover policy
Provides a lump sum payment if you experience a specified traumatic health event 2 such as a stroke, heart attack or cancer diagnosis.

## FAQs

## How does Income Protection work?
If you're unable to work due to illness or injury 1 , Income Protection provides a monthly payment of up to 70% of your income that could help to cover:
Everyday living expenses, including utilities, school fees or childcare
Other financial support needs, so you can focus on getting back on your feet

## What doesn’t Income Protection cover?
Some exclusions which aren’t covered by Income Protection include if disablement 1 is due to intentional self-inflicted injury or any such attempt by you, criminal activity (including periods of incarceration), active military service or normal pregnancy.
For more information on exclusions, specific definitions and how they apply to you, refer to the Product Disclosure Statement (PDS) .

## Who can apply for Income Protection?
You can apply if you’re:
Aged 18–59 (some occupations are only to age 54)
An Australian citizen or permanent resident who is in Australia
A New Zealand citizen living and working in Australia permanently (on a Subclass 444 visa)
A permanent employee or are self-employed working at least 25 hours per week
Not performing heavy manual work (Occupation Category E)

## How much Income Protection do I need?
The amount of cover someone may need can vary depending on individual circumstances. While there’s no one-size-fits-all answer, there are some common factors people often consider when thinking about their insurance needs:
Their monthly expenses
The amount of time they'd need support for any other sources of income they may have (including leave payments)

## Are Income Protection premiums tax-deductible?
You can generally claim some or all of your Income Protection premiums (paid outside of super) as a deduction against your taxable income.
