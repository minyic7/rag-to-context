Title: Types of home loans

## Variable rate home loans
With a variable rate loan, your repayments will vary depending on when the interest rate rises and falls. If rates go up, your repayments do as well. If rates go down, your repayments may fall too. Home loan options include:

## Digi Home Loan

## Standard Variable Rate
Standard Variable Rate
A standard variable rate home loan with up to 99 Everyday Offset accounts. Freedom to split your loan, unlimited additional repayments, redraws 4 , option to make Interest Only 5 payments. Negotiate your rate when you apply.

## Simple Home Loan
A competitive variable rate home loan and choice to access two Everyday Offset accounts 2&8 . Unlimited additional repayments, redraws 4 , and option to make Interest Only 5 payments. Negotiate your rate when you apply.

## Fixed rate home loans
The main advantage of a fixed rate home loan is certainty. You can lock in or ‘fix’ your interest rate for a certain period of time – typically between one and five years – and plan for the future, knowing that your repayments will stay the same during that time. Home loan options include:

## Fixed Rate
A fixed rate home loan with the flexibility to add in other features. Additional repayments can be made up to $10,000 in a fixed year without incurring additional charges.

## Green Home Loan
A low, secured fixed rate home loan with no establishment fee, monthly loan service fee or early repayment fee.

## Borrowing options

## Split loan
You can choose to split your home loan by nominating a proportion of the loan as fixed and proportion as variable.

## Construction loan
Access money progressively as you complete different stages with a construction loan.

## Low deposit options
If you have a low deposit, there are still pathways to help you achieve your home ownership goals.

## Bridging loan
Buy a new home before you’ve sold your existing one by using a bridging loan.

## Repayments
Understand repayments and how to best manage them can help you make the most of your home loan.

## Interest Only loans
Understanding some important things about an Interest Only home loan can help you decide whether it’s the right payment option for you.

## Fixed or variable home loan: which is right for you?
Which is right for you? Choosing to go with a home loan that comes with a fixed or variable rate of interest will depend upon your personal and financial circumstances.

## Home loans for business owners
Are you a sole trader or self-employed? Our dedicated Home Lending Specialists are here to help business owners like you find the right home loan.

## Paying off your home loan
The two biggest components of your home loan repayments are typically the principal component and the interest component. Depending on your situation, one of these types of loans may be more suitable than the other.
