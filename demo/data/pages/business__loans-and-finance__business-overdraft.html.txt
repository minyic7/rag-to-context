## What's a Business Overdraft?
A Business Overdraft is a short-term cash flow solution linked to your Business Transaction Account which you can access whenever you need, up to an approved limit. They’re often used to cover unexpected expenses or seasonal fluctuations.

## Frequently asked questions

## What are the benefits of a business overdraft?
Benefits of a business overdraft include:
Quick access to funds
Used to cover short-term cash flow gaps like unexpected expenses or seasonal fluctuations
No fixed repayments, repay when money hits your account
Only pay interest on what you use

## How do I withdraw money from my overdraft?
You can access the money through your Business Transaction Account, using the usual methods: NetBank , the CommBank app , CommBiz , at CommBank ATMs, in branch, over the phone or through EFTPOS and cheques.

## What's the difference between a loan and an overdraft?
With a loan, you’re given the amount in a lump sum and charged interest on the whole amount, which you'll pay off over a period of time. Loans are generally taken when you need to make a purchase upfront and in full.
An overdraft is linked to your transaction account and allows you to access additional funds when your account balance reaches zero. You’re only charged interest once you use the money, and you’re only charged interest on the amount you use. Overdrafts are a way of making sure money is there if you should need it. For example, to manage unexpected expenses.

## What’s the difference between a business overdraft and a line of credit?
Both options are used to manage cash flow. While a business overdraft is linked to your Business Transaction Account and often used for short-term cash flow needs, a line of credit is a separate account or loan, accessed independently and often used for medium to long-term needs.
