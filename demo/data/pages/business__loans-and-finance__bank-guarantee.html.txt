Title: Bank Guarantee for business banking customers

## What's a Bank Guarantee?
A Bank Guarantee is an alternative to providing a deposit or bond directly to a supplier, vendor or landlord, known as a Favouree (or beneficiary). Your Favouree has the certainty of knowing they’ll be paid, while you benefit from interest earned on your cash held in an eligible CommBank deposit account. Guarantees are often used for: Security deposits, such as rental bonds to landlords or real estate agents to lease premises Contract performance guarantees for supplied goods and services

## Frequently asked questions

## Who can apply for a Bank Guarantee?
Guarantees may be available to business customers, government entities and not-for-profit organisations. You're more likely to be approved if:
You haven't been overdrawn or had your account in arrears in the past 3 months
You haven't been declared bankrupt in the last 5 years
You haven't had any accounts in collections in the past 6 months

## Who can be a Favouree?
This is the third party you provide the guarantee to. A Favouree must:
Have a valid ABN or ACN (if the Favouree is not an individual)
Not be a superannuation fund, standalone trust (unless the trustee is also named), partnership (unless each of the partners are named) or unincorporated entity as these are not legal entities
View our Bank Guarantee: Favouree fact sheet for more information.

## What can I use to secure a Bank Guarantee?
You can secure your guarantee with:
Cash kept in a CommBank Business Investment Account , Business Online Saver or Term Deposit so you can earn interest on it until your guarantee expires
Commercial or residential property ( contact us or your Relationship Manager to find out how )
We release your security when your guarantee expires, unless we've had to use it to pay a claim to your Favouree.

## How long is a Bank Guarantee valid for?
You can agree on an expiration date with the Favouree. This date marks the end of your obligations to the Favouree and the bank, even if you lose contact with the Favouree. It also ensures that at some point you’ll be able to get your security back, as long as there’s no claim made by the Favouree.
It’s a good idea to set an expiration date, otherwise the Favouree could make a claim after your obligation has ended and the bank is obliged to use your security to pay the Favouree.

## How can I cancel a Bank Guarantee?
To cancel your Bank Guarantee, one of the following needs to occur:
You or the Favouree returns the original guarantee to us
The Favouree cancels the guarantee by completing a Cancellation Form (PDF 127KB)
The guarantee expires
The Favouree has been paid
Learn more about how to cancel, amend or claim a Bank Guarantee .

## How can I amend a Bank Guarantee?
If you need to make a change to a guarantee that’s already been issued, you can:
Contact your Relationship Manager (if you have one)
Call our Business Banking team on 13 1998
Visit your local branch
The Favouree must complete an Amendment Form (PDF 580KB) for certain request types.
A redocumentation fee of $250 will apply.
Learn more about how to cancel, amend or claim a Bank Guarantee .

## How can a Favouree claim a Bank Guarantee?
To make a claim, the Favouree must provide:
A completed Claim Form (PDF 144KB) or Letter of Demand
The original Bank Guarantee document
Learn more about how to cancel, amend or claim a Bank Guarantee .

## How do I set up a Bank Guarantee for an Assurance of Support agreement?
You cannot set up a bank guarantee for an Assurance of Support online – you'll need to apply in branch.
If you’re supporting someone’s visa application through an Assurance of Support, Services Australia may ask you to provide a bank guarantee and term deposit. Do not arrange these until you receive a written request from Services Australia to do so.
Once you receive the letter, you’ll need to visit a CommBank branch. Bring the letter with you and have the required funds ready (the letter will specify the amount).
For more information about Assurance of Support agreements, visit the Services Australia website .
