## Frequently asked questions

## What's an online payment gateway?
If your business sells products or services using a website, you’ll need a payment gateway to receive digital payments. A payment gateway integrates with your website’s shopping cart to accept and encrypt customer card details. It then authenticates the details and payment, before moving the money into your settlement account.

## How do online payments work?
Customers purchase goods or services using your website. Once they proceed to pay, they enter their credit or debit card details and are authenticated. The money is temporarily held to allow for authorisation and fraud checks, before being moved into your linked settlement account.

## How do I setup an online payment gateway?
Getting started is simple:
Create a business account with your chosen provider
Verify your business with your ID and bank details
Integrate the gateway with your eCommerce platform or invoicing system
Test transactions in the testing environment provided by the payment gateway provider. This allows you to simulate real payment transactions without processing actual money
Go live and start accepting secure online payments.
We have an integration support team that can help you set-up your CommBank payment gateway.

## How can I make sure online payments are secure?
You can setup your eCommerce platform with EMV 3D Secure. Doing so will mean customers’ card details are authenticated and you have increased protection against fraud-related disputes and chargebacks. EMV 3D Secure also uses tokenisation so that card details are securely stored.
Your payment gateway host (e.g. a bank) securely manages the card data so you don’t have to.

## How does EMV 3D Secure work?
EMV 3D Secure (3DS 2) checks a number of data points to identify and authenticate customers. Based on the calculated transaction risk, the cardholder may be prompted to verify their ID eg. with a one-time password or biometric authentication using your banking app.

## What information do I need to apply?
Have these details handy:
Business contact information, including phone and email
Australian Business Number (ABN), business structure and registered address
Financial information, including yearly turnover (or estimated average online sales), average transaction value, start date
Details of every director, partner or trustee depending on your business structure. This includes birth date, residency and occupation information

## What should I look for when choosing an online payment gateway solution for a small business?
When choosing an online payment gateway solution for a small business, prioritise a smooth, mobile-friendly checkout and reliable performance to build trust and reduce cart abandonment. Consider a provider with strong encryption and fraud protection, and compare transaction fees and other charges to ensure these are realistic for your business.

## How to set up online payments and recurring billing for a subscription business?
Define your subscription model, including what you're selling, how often you'll bill customers, and whether fees are flat, tiered or usage based. Then choose a payment gateway that supports recurring payments, allows card-on-file or bank account authorisation, and integrates with your systems.

## What's the difference between a payment gateway and a merchant account?
A payment gateway integrates with your website’s shopping cart to accept and encrypt customer card details. It then authenticates the details and payment, before moving the money into your settlement account. A merchant account is a type of bank account in which funds from your customers’ cards or electronic payments are temporarily held for authorisation and fraud checks, before being transferred into your regular business bank account.
