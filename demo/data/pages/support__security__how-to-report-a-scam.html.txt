## How to report a scam
If you think you’ve been scammed, follow these steps immediately:​ Stop all communications with the suspected scammer​ Change your password in NetBank or CommBiz and change your card PIN ​ Lock your card ​and check your digital wallets. Go to card settings in the CommBank app to check and deregister any devices in the list that you don’t recognise Message us in the CommBank app 24/7 or call us on 13 2221 , or CommBiz 13 2339

## Watch out for follow up scams
Scammers will often try to contact you again. Be alert to offers “to recover your lost money,” “track criminals” or “charge a fee to return money.” Remember, if you receive a call from someone claiming to be from CommBank, ask the person to use CallerCheck so you can verify their identity in the CommBank app and be confident you are authentically talking to us.

## How we can help
Falling victim to a scam can have life-changing consequences. It’s important to know support is available to help you, should you ever need it.
Customer support service
If you would like to talk to someone about your personal wellbeing, CommBank offers a confidential, phone-based, counselling service. You can make an appointment by calling Customer Support Service on 1300 360 793 .
Financial assist If you’re having difficulty meeting your CommBank loan or credit card repayments as a result of being the victim of a scam, our Financial assist team is here to support you:
Message us in the CommBank app
Call us: 1300 720 814 or business customers call us on 13 2607 8am to 9pm Monday to Friday (AEST) 9am to 2pm Saturday (AEST)

## Remember 3 simple steps:Stop. Check. Reject.
Stop Does a call, email or text seem off? The best thing to do is stop. Take a breath. Real organisations won’t put you under pressure to act instantly. Check Ask someone you trust or contact the organisation the message claims to be from.​ Reject If you’re unsure, hang up on the caller, delete the email, block the phone number. Change your passwords
