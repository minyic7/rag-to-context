## Eligibility & benefits overview

## Everyday Business set eligibility
You qualify for the Everyday Business benefit set if you:
Hold one or more eligible business transaction accounts at the end of the previous calendar month
Have made at least 5 eligible, completed outbound transactions in total from any eligible business transaction accounts in the previous calendar month
Eligible business transaction accounts:
Business Transaction Account
Stream Working Capital Transaction Account
For more information on eligibility for the Everyday Business benefit set, refer to the CommBank Yello for Business Terms and Conditions .

## Business Growth set eligibility
You qualify for the Business Growth benefit set if you:
Hold one or more eligible business transaction accounts at the end of the previous calendar month
Have made at least 5 eligible, completed outbound transactions in total from any eligible business transaction accounts in the previous calendar month
The same entity (that you are linked to) that holds the eligible business transaction account or accounts also holds at least one eligible business loan or finance product at the end of the previous calendar month
Eligible business transaction accounts:
Business Transaction Account
Stream Working Capital Transaction Account
Eligible business loan or finance products:
Asset Finance (Car & Equipment Finance)
Stream Working Capital
For more information on eligibility for the Business Growth benefit set, refer to the CommBank Yello for Business Terms and Conditions .

## What could impact my eligibility?
An authorised operator will no longer be eligible on your behalf for CommBank Yello for Business if one or more of the following conditions apply:
We decide that we no longer wish to maintain a banking relationship with you or the authorised operator
The authorised operator is an authorised operator on multiple business products which are held by more than twenty-five different business customers
The authorised operator is under the age of 18
We are notified that the authorised operator is deceased
The addresses we hold for you, or an authorised operator, are outside of Australia
You do not hold any eligible business transaction accounts
You or the authorised operator do not meet or no longer meet our ‘Know Your Customer’ requirements (being the customer identification procedures and customer due diligence we are obliged to undertake under the law relating to our customers and account authorised operators)
An authorised operator will no longer be able to access CommBank Yello for Business if the authorised operator opts out. If there are multiple authorised operators but one of you chooses to opt out of CommBank Yello for Business, the other authorised operators will still have access to the program if the business eligibility criteria are met.
A business loan or finance product will no longer be considered as eligible if it is either:
Refinanced into another business loan product that is not eligible
Refinanced to another bank
For more information on eligibility exclusions, view the CommBank Yello for Business Terms and Conditions .

## Frequently asked questions

## Can anyone get access to CommBank Yello for Business?
You’ll be able to access CommBank Yello for Business if you’ve met the eligibility criteria.
If you have met the eligibility criteria and offers and discounts aren’t appearing for you, refer to FAQ 'Why are no benefits appearing for me?’

## How long do my CommBank Yello for Business benefits last?
Once you've met the eligibility criteria, you'll get access to offers and discounts for the next 3 months. For example, if you meet the eligibility criteria in January, you’ll get offers from February until the end of April.
Each time you meet the eligibility criteria, your 3 months of benefits will renew. So, if you meet the criteria again in February, you’ll get offers until the end of May.
If you meet the exclusion criteria, you'll lose access to benefits in the following month. Refer to Eligibility & benefits overview 'What could impact my eligibility?' for more information.

## Why are no benefits appearing for me?
First, check that you have the most recent version of the CommBank app and that you’re using your business profile.
If offers still aren’t showing:
You may be ineligible
You may have opted out of CommBank Yello for Business
Refer to Eligibility & benefits overview 'What could impact my eligibility?'

## How do I set up a business profile in the CommBank app?
Tap the profile icon in the top-left corner of your home screen
Scroll to Settings , tap Set up business profile , then Set up now
Name your business profile, then tap Next
Select the accounts you’d like to include
Once your business profile is set up, a toggle will appear next to your profile icon. You can then simply switch between your business and personal profiles with a tap.

## How do I opt out of CommBank Yello for Business?
If you’re an authorised operator, follow these steps to opt out:
Open the CommBank app
Switch to your business profile
Opting out of CommBank Yello for Business doesn’t opt you out of CommBank Yello offers for our retail customers.
If there are multiple authorised operators of an eligible business transaction account but one chooses to opt out of CommBank Yello for Business, the other will still have access to the program as long as the business eligibility criteria is met.

## I've opted out. Can I opt back in?
You're currently unable to opt back in to CommBank Yello for Business.

## I've been notified about CommBank Yello for Business. How do I know it's not a scam?
Log on to the CommBank app, go to your business profile and then tap on CBA Yello . You’ll be able to view the benefits your business is eligible for.
Protecting you is our priority. Remember, we’ll never ask you for any banking information, like your account or logon details.
Visit CommBank Safe to learn more about how we protect you and what you can do to protect yourself.

## What are the tax implications of CommBank Yello for Business benefits?
There may be tax implications of receiving a benefit. You should check with your accountant or tax adviser to discuss your individual circumstances.
