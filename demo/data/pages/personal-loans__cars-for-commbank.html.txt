## How does a salary packaged EV subscription work?
The EV subscription salary packaging plan available via the Cars for CommBank platform is provided by Origin. It’s a flexible month-to-month plan that bundles your vehicle, insurance, maintenance and registration into one entire package. Plus, no lock-in contracts so you can switch, return or upgrade your car with just 30 days notice. This means you can drive away in an EV without the upfront costs and responsibilities of ownership. You can learn more and subscribe to an EV here . Salary packaging, also known as salary sacrificing or remuneration packaging, involves an agreement to receive less take-home income, in return for your employer paying for specific benefits of a similar value. This is an arrangement made between you and your employer – and the available benefits (such as cars or electronic devices) often depends on what your organisation offers.
You’ll need to arrange your salary package in advance, as you can’t package your salary after you’ve earned it. For more information on salary package arrangements, visit the Australian Tax Office (ATO) website .

## What are some tips for buying a used car?
Buying a used car requires careful planning, research and consideration to ensure you make a well-informed decision. This is a guide to help you through the process: Planning and research
Determine your budget including ongoing costs like insurance, registration, fuel and maintenance.
Research reliable vehicle models that match your needs.
Compare market values across different sellers and locations.
Consider conditional approval for car finance to help you shop with confidence.
Use the comparison tool in Cars for CommBank to assess up to three vehicles that suit your needs and budget.
If you’re considering an EV, use CommBank's EV calculator to compare running costs between EV, petrol, diesel and hybrid options.
Explore car loan rates and use the repayment calculator to plan your budget.
Confirm the car's history with the dealer or manufacturer.
Check for any outstanding finance.
Inspect the vehicle thoroughly in person.
Review all documentation carefully.
Ensure registration and transfer papers are correct.
Purchase the right insurance coverage before driving.

## How do I know if the car I'm buying is considered cheap?
A car is considered cheap if its price is significantly lower than its market value. This can be determined by researching similar vehicles online and considering factors like age, mileage, condition and features. You could use the car comparison feature on Cars for CommBank to compare up to three vehicles side by side. This allows you to compare details like pricing, key features and specifications to help you evaluate options within your budget.
